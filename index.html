Books I've Read

Algorithms to Live By

by Brian Christian & Tom Griffiths

This fascinating book bridges the gap between computer science and human decision-making. It explores how algorithms can help us make better decisions in everyday life, from finding a parking spot to organizing our homes.

Key takeaways:

  • Understanding optimal stopping problems helps in making better decisions about when to stop searching
  • Sorting algorithms can be applied to organizing physical spaces
  • Cache management principles can improve our daily routines

Rating: 4.5/5 - A must-read for anyone interested in decision-making and computer science.

So Good They Can't Ignore You

by Cal Newport

Cal Newport challenges the conventional "follow your passion" advice and presents a compelling case for developing rare and valuable skills instead. This book completely changed my perspective on career development.

Key takeaways:

  • Passion is often the result of mastery, not the starting point
  • The importance of deliberate practice in skill development
  • How to build career capital and gain more control over your work

Rating: 5/5 - A game-changing book for career development and personal growth.

Atomic Habits

by James Clear

A revolutionary guide to building good habits and breaking bad ones. This book provides practical strategies for making tiny changes that lead to remarkable results.

Key takeaways:

  • The power of 1% improvements and compound growth
  • How to create an environment that supports good habits
  • The importance of identity-based habits

Rating: 5/5 - Essential reading for personal development and habit formation.

Deep Work

by Cal Newport

In an age of constant distraction, this book makes a compelling case for the importance of focused, distraction-free work. It provides practical strategies for developing the ability to focus deeply.

Key takeaways:

  • The value of deep work in the knowledge economy
  • Strategies for eliminating distractions
  • How to build a deep work routine

Rating: 4.8/5 - A must-read for knowledge workers and anyone seeking to improve their focus.

The Pragmatic Programmer

by Andrew Hunt & David Thomas

A timeless guide to software development that goes beyond specific technologies. This book provides practical advice and wisdom for becoming a better programmer.

Key takeaways:

  • DRY (Don't Repeat Yourself) principle and its importance
  • How to write flexible, maintainable code
  • Best practices for debugging and testing

Rating: 4.7/5 - Essential reading for software developers at any level.

Designing Data-Intensive Applications

by Martin Kleppmann

An in-depth exploration of the principles behind building reliable, scalable, and maintainable data systems. This book is a comprehensive guide to modern distributed systems.

Key takeaways:

  • Understanding distributed systems and their challenges
  • Database internals and trade-offs
  • Designing for reliability and scalability

Rating: 4.9/5 - A masterpiece for anyone working with data systems.
